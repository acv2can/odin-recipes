Pastafrola

Description

The Pastafrola is an argentinian pie that consists of a regular pie crust filled with quince jelly and topped with grated coconut.

Today I´ll show you how it´s done so you can make one at home and share it with your loved ones, but keep in mind that this pastry is highly addictive, so if you're on a diet, you should be careful.

Ingredients

  • 200gr of butter
  • 100gr of sugar
  • Half a tablespoon of vanilla extract
  • 3 eggs
  • 500gr of self-rising flour
  • 500gr of quince jelly
  • 2 tablespoons of cognac
  • 150ml of warm water
  • 10gr of grated coconut

Steps

  1. Mix the butter, which has to be at room temperature, with the sugar.
  2. Add the eggs one by one to the previous mix along with the vanilla extract and whisk until everything is amalgamated.
  3. Add the flour to the mix and try to fraisage everything together, so the dough is perfectly formed. Do NOT knead it, or you will ruin it.
  4. Once the dough is made, wrap it in film paper and take it to the fridge for an hour.
  5. In the meantime, add the warm water and the cognac to the quince jelly and smash it, so it ends up being a mush.
  6. After the dough has rested in the fridge, take it out and stretch it so you can cover a pie mold with it. Leave out about 1/4 of the dough for the decorations.
  7. Fill the pie with the quince mush then stretch the leftover dough, cut it in stripes and put it on top of the filled pie creating a lattice.
  8. Finally, put the Pastafrola in the oven at 180 Celsius degrees for 40 minutes. After you take it out, sprinkle some grated coconut on top.
